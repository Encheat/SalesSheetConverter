Some documents will be filled out with tally marks instead of numbers.
Interpret tally marks using the standard counting system:
- One vertical line = 1
- Two vertical lines = 2
- Four vertical lines = 4
- Four vertical lines with a 5th line crossing over them diagonally in either direction = 5
- Once 5 tallies are counted, it's a complete group. 
- Additional tallies after a group count as their own group, up to a max of 5.
- No tally mark group will have less than 5 tallies.
For each tally cell:
Step 1:
Count the number of complete crossed groups (5 each).
Step 2:
Count any remaining uncrossed vertical lines.
Step 3:
Add them together.
Example:
2 crossed groups + 3 vertical lines = 13
Always return the final numeric value, never the tally marks themselves.
If several fields use tally marks, assume all other fields do to unless they have a symbol that must be translated as a number, like 31.
